2DR Method

A 2x2 method proposed by Harrison O'Reibow

2DR is a 2x2 method designed to be used alongside another intermediate method like Ortega. I called it 2DR because it is similar to domino reduction for 3x3, but on a 2x2.

Steps:

  1. Make a V (¾ of a side) using opposite colours. This step is very often already done. I would recommend using a different method such as Ortega if the V isn't skipped, because four steps is too many.
  2. Use TOLL (24 algorithms, 15 excluding mirrors) to orient the five remaining pieces. Alternatively, you could make an entire layer in step 1, then use OLL, perhaps as a beginner version.
  3. Intuitively separate the opposite colours onto their respective sides. Do this as you would for Square-1 or 2x2x3 corner orientation.
  4. Use Ortega PBL to solve the rest of the cube. The PBL case can be influenced by changing how you do the separation.

Stats:

No. steps: 4 (Opposite V, TOLL, Separate, PBL)
No. algs: Total: 36
TOLL (23),
Separtaion (5),
PBL (8)
Avg. moves: ≈16HTM

Pros

  • Lower movecount than Ortega
  • Easy lookahead due to short TOLLs and intuitive separation
  • Alg count can be reduced by mirroring TOLL- to get TOLL+

Cons

  • Not worth it if there is no V already made
  • Higher algorithm count than Ortega
